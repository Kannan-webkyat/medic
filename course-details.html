Bachelor of Science (B.Sc)(Nursing)

The Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSc Nursing) program is an undergraduate degree pathway designed to equip individuals with the knowledge and skills necessary for a fulfilling career in nursing. Students enrolled in BSc Nursing programs undergo a comprehensive curriculum that covers a wide range of subjects, including anatomy, physiology, pharmacology, microbiology, psychology, and nursing theory. Through a combination of classroom instruction, laboratory work, and clinical practice, students learn to provide compassionate and evidence-based care to patients across the lifespan. Clinical training in hospitals, community health centers, and other healthcare settings allows students to apply their knowledge in real-world scenarios, developing critical thinking, communication, and clinical decision-making skills essential for nursing practice. Upon successful completion of the BSc Nursing program, graduates are eligible to take the licensing examination to become registered nurses (RNs). Armed with a solid foundation in nursing science and practice, RNs play a crucial role in promoting health, preventing illness, and caring for individuals with acute and chronic health conditions. They work collaboratively with other healthcare professionals to assess patient needs, develop care plans, administer treatments, and educate patients and their families about health management. With opportunities for specialization in areas such as pediatric nursing, psychiatric nursing, critical care nursing, and more, BSc Nursing graduates have diverse career options and can make meaningful contributions to healthcare delivery and patient outcomes.

Details of BSc Nursing Program

  • Duration

    4 Years

  • Eligibility

    Candidates to have studied science subjects such as biology, chemistry, & physics during their 10th & +2.

  • Minimum Age Requirement

    17-18 years old

  • Minimum Percentage

    Around 50%-60%

  • Jobs

    Registered Nurse (RN), Nurse Educator, Nurse Administrator, Public Health Nurse, Clinical Nurse Specialist (CNS) or Nurse Practitioner (NP)
